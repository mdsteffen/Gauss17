Construction of a Regular Pentagon with Compass and Straightedge

Below are two methods for constructing a regular pentagon with compass and straightedge. Method 1 is the more traditional method while method 2 uses a Carlyle circle technique for a more efficient construction.

Method 1:

Steps:

  1. Draw circle with radius R centered at point O
  2. Draw a diameter, intersecting the circle at points A and B
  3. Create a perpendicular line to AB at point O, intersecting the circle at point P1.
  4. Find the midpoint of line BO, call this point C.
  5. Draw the line CP1. (optional - can help with next step)
  6. Bisect angle P1CO, intersecting line OP1 at point D.
  7. Draw a line perpendicular to OP1 at point D, intersecting the circle at point P2.
  8. Center compass at point P2 with length P1P2 and mark point P3 on the circle.
  9. Continue, marking points P4 and P5.
  10. Points P1-P5 form the pentagon.

Method 2:

Steps:

  1. Draw circle with radius R centered at point O
  2. Draw a diameter, intersecting the circle at points A and B
  3. Create a perpendicular line to AB at point O, intersecting the circle at point P1.
  4. Find the midpoint of line BO, call this point C.
  5. Center compass at point C with length CP1, draw arc to intersect line AB at point D (this is the Carlyle circle).
  6. Center compass at point P1 with length P1D and mark point P2 on the circle.
  7. Continue, marking points P3, P4, and P5.
  8. Points P1-P5 form the pentagon.
